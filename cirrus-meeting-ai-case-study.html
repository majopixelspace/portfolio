The brief

A salesperson's day doesn't fit in a table.

Cirrus Insight helps revenue teams run meetings that matter — reviews, renewals, discovery calls. But its power lived in dense desktop tables and multi-click flows. On a phone, between calls, a rep just wants to know one thing: what should I do next, and who is engaging with me?

The mandate from the product team was not a re-skin. It was a rethink of how meeting intelligence — preparation, live capture and follow-up — surfaces across the calendar, the record and the phone, without drowning the user in configuration.

“The only way to survive with tables on mobile is to not use tables.”

— guiding principle from the design reviews

Density without hierarchy

Seven-column tables and stacked icons meant every meeting looked equally urgent. Reps couldn't scan a week and know what needed them.

One-size configuration

Data-heavy and data-light orgs shared one rigid record layout. Admin-level customization carried too much cognitive cost for the person in the field.

Fragmented follow-through

Prep notes, live transcript and action items lived in separate tools. The thread from “before the call” to “after the call” kept breaking.

The concept

Records span meetings. Meetings are moments in time.

The organizing insight, from product lead Mike: an account and its attendees don't change from call to call, but what happens in a call does. Separating the persistent record from the temporal event gave the whole product its shape — and let one blade adapt to three states.

PHASE 01 · BEFORE

Pre-Meeting

Lands on → Prep
  • Account & attendees pinned above the line — the record that spans meetings.
  • AI talking points and recent activity to walk in prepared.
  • Insights & Actions show an empty state — nothing has happened yet.
Why Prep first: before a call, preparation is the only useful surface.
PHASE 02 · DURING

In-Meeting

Lands on → Insights
  • AI Coach retrieves answers from the account knowledge base in real time.
  • Live transcript with a pulsing “LIVE” marker and note-taking side-by-side.
  • Progressive reveal — content appears softly, never yanks attention from the call.
Why Insights first: mid-call, the live thread is the value.
PHASE 03 · AFTER

Post-Meeting

Lands on → Insights → Next
  • AI summary: key decisions, context highlights, risks — with timecodes.
  • Next Actions kanban: Do Now / Do Next / Do Later, AI-assisted.
  • CRM-sourced attendee provenance (Salesforce vs LinkedIn) for trust.
Why it carries over: the completed record feeds the next prep.

Insights · mobile

Tables become cards

On the phone the seven-column table dissolves into scannable cards — a big “opens” number, a location, and the only two actions that matter: Call or Email.

Signature feature

Seven meeting states, told without icon clutter.

The hardest problem on the calendar: communicate a meeting's recording & AI status at a glance, on mobile, for seven distinct states — without the “stack another icon” trap. The answer was a three-layer visual language.

LAYER 01

Colored left border

A 3px bar carries the Meeting-AI status. Color is the fastest thing to scan across a week.

10:00Client Onboarding
LAYER 02

Sparkle glyph

A four-point star marks AI presence — and only appears when AI is involved. It pulses when live.

9:00Q2 Review
LAYER 03

Camera glyph

An independent red camera signals a recording exists — orthogonal to AI, so states compose cleanly.

Because each layer is independent, a new state is a new border color — never another icon. On desktop the meeting reveals a hover card with the matching control (a toggle to enable AI, a button to view highlights, a “live now” indicator). On mobile, that same card slides up as a bottom sheet. One model, two input methods.

Design decisions

Every choice, and its reason.

Prototypes are arguments. Each of these was made to answer a real question raised in the reviews with Mike, Malikica and Hank — and each shipped as an A/B/C option so the client could feel the trade-off, not just read it.

01

Move accounts & attendees “above the line.”

Decision

A static record header — company, key metrics, attendees — sits above the tab system and never scrolls away. Tabs (Prep / Insights / Actions) carry only what changes.

Why

It encodes the core mental model: records persist, meetings are events. The parts that span meetings should feel permanent.

“The accounts and the attendees… those records don't change. They span meetings.”Mike · Product lead
02

Land on the tab that fits the moment.

Decision

The blade opens on Prep before a meeting, Insights during and after. No tab is ever locked — the default just matches intent.

Why

State-dependent defaults remove a click at exactly the moment the user is busiest, while keeping the full surface reachable.

“Between these three tabs you'd land on whichever was most relevant depending on the state of the meeting.”Malikica · Product
03

Live insights that don't hijack the call.

Decision

During a meeting, insights appear through progressive reveal — soft fade-ins, topic pills, timestamped moments, and a gentle “Listening…” pulse instead of a firehose.

Why

The screen is a co-pilot, not a distraction. The rep should be able to glance, not get pulled out of the conversation.

“If it's generating live, how does it not distract the user if the call's happening?”Malikica · Product
04

Don't reflow tables on mobile — replace them.

Decision

Below 768px, data tables are fully hidden and rebuilt as purpose-made cards showing only the fields that drive the next action. Everything else moves behind a tap.

Why

A reflowed table is still a table. Field reduction — not CSS gymnastics — is what makes mobile usable between meetings.

“The only way to survive with tables on mobile is to not use tables.”Design review principle
05

Customization the user owns — not the admin.

Decision

Block 6 moved the config panel from admin-level to a personal “Customize My View,” plus inline hide (×) and collapse on each card. Low cognitive cost, not Office-grade settings.

Why

Schema-driven layouts must serve both data-heavy and data-light orgs. Letting the rep quietly hide what they don't use beats forcing one canonical layout.

Not requested → not built. “Log Activity” and “Edit” buttons were removed because they weren't in the client's vision — only what was asked ships.Working principle with the client
Process

Six blocks, small bites, fast loops.

Work moved in numbered blocks, each closing a set of tasks from a biweekly review and opening the next. Every block shipped as runnable HTML the client could click — not a deck.

Where a decision was contested, it shipped as parallel A / B / C / D variants in the same build, so the team could compare a scrollable-pill filter against a bottom tab bar against a dropdown — in their own hands.

ABCD A = Hybrid · B = Shortcuts · C = Dashboard · D = Briefing

Outcome

A clickable product, and a spec for the real one.

The prototypes became the shared reference language between design, product and engineering — every screen agreed on before a line of production code, and a documented path to build it for real.
